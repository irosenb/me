Morse

Morse lets your audience be heard.

Meetups like NYTM are usually a mess when it comes to the Q&A. People in the top row don't usually get called on to ask questions. And when someone asks a question, nobody can hear them.

But everyone now has a phone. Why don't we use them?

That's what Morse is. The way it works is that the conference is given a phone number. When it's Q&A time, people who have questions call the number and ask their question. In real-time, their voice is going over the loudspeakers.

I'm still working on it. Let me know if you're interested in it.

MapHunt

MapHunt is mapping the world's creators.

Coulton Vento and I built this at the Product Hunt hackathon using Ruby on Rails. We were 1 of 7 projects chosen to present in front of the entire hackathon.

The next day we were featured on the Product Hunt site. That drew over 16,000 views and comments from figures like Alexis Ohanian and Ryan Hoover.

Presented MapHunt at Mashape's first meetup. I talked about MapHunt's past, present, and future. Got a lot of awesome feedback about MapHunt.

Made a newsletter for MapHunt. You should subscribe if you're interested in following along MapHunt's progress.

Medicine

An iOS app and Arduino prototype that sends a push notification to your phone when you haven't taken your medicine.

Built by myself using Arduino and Swift. Prototyped it with my pillbox. Uses Bluetooth Low Energy to detect when you're too far away.

Medicine is not available for prime time yet. It's not on the App Store, nor is it on Kickstarter. I want to take the prototype and turn it into something more. Let me know if you're interested.

Guminar

Redesigned Gumroad's now-defunct webinar (of course, without their permission). Built with Ruby on Rails. Used the GoToWebinar API and Gumroad's Ping feature to simulate registering for a webinar.

Showed it to the Gumroad team, they approved :)

Cleanist

Cleanist declutters your reading list by cleaning out the articles that you don't read. Almost like a self-destruct button for Pocket.

Built in Ruby on Rails.

Catupult

Sends you a picture of a cat when you're feeling down. Built with a fellow Flatiron School student at a Jawbone hackathon in New York.

Uses both the Twillio and Jawbone APIs. Check it out on Github.
